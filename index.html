  • Ibrahim Rauza, a mausoleum built during the 17th century, is a striking example of Deccan Islamic architecture. It was commissioned by Ibrahim Adil Shah II, the ruler of the Adil Shahi dynasty, to serve as a final resting place for himself and his queen. The structure is a harmonious blend of Persian, Indian, and Turkish architectural styles, showcasing intricate carvings, delicate screens, and an awe-inspiring dome that graces the skyline of Vijayapura.

    The monument's minarets, with their delicate geometric patterns, reach towards the heavens, creating an aura of spiritual serenity. The central dome, often referred to as the "Queen of Domes," is an engineering marvel that captivates visitors with its size and symmetry. It is said that the construction of the dome was so precise that one can hear echoes seven times when standing directly beneath it. The main chamber houses the tombs of Ibrahim Adil Shah II and his queen, Taj Sultana, evoking a sense of historical reverence and cultural richness.
